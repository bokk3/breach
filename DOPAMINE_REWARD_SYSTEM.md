# Dopamine-Driven Reward System Design

## Core Psychological Principles

### 1. Variable Reward Schedule
- Unpredictable rewards create stronger dopamine responses
- Mix guaranteed and random rewards
- Create "near-miss" moments that keep players engaged

### 2. Immediate Feedback
- Instant visual and audio confirmation
- Numbers go up = brain happy
- Cascading effects and chain reactions

### 3. Achievement Milestones
- Short-term goals (combo streaks)
- Medium-term goals (level ups)
- Long-term goals (achievements/badges)

### 4. Loss Aversion
- Fear of losing combo creates tension
- Risk/reward decisions
- "One more try" psychology

## Psychological Hooks

### Anticipation
- Power-up spawn animations
- "Something good is coming" indicators
- Countdown timers create urgency

### Surprise
- Random critical hits
- Unexpected bonuses
- Rare node spawns

### Accomplishment
- Achievement unlocks
- Level ups with fanfare
- Milestone celebrations

### Social Proof
- Leaderboard rankings
- Share achievements
- Compare with friends

### Collection
- Badge collection
- Achievement hunting
- "Gotta catch 'em all" mentality

### Mastery
- Skill improvement visible
- Higher combos over time
- Difficulty progression

## Success Metrics

- **Session Length**: Target 10+ minutes average
- **Return Rate**: 60%+ players return next day
- **Completion Rate**: 40%+ finish a full game
- **Engagement**: 3+ games per session
- **Viral Coefficient**: Players share achievements
